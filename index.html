Foodies Milestones

Early Ideation

The early ideation phase was crucial for laying the groundwork for the Foodies project. During this phase, we conducted brainstorming sessions to generate ideas, sketch rough wireframes, and outline the core objectives of the platform. We explored various features that could enhance user engagement, including an interactive map for discovering food trucks, a calendar for upcoming events, and booking options for private events.

This sketch (see Image 4) was my initial idea and design for the Foodies project.

Research & Inspiration

During the research phase, I explored various projects that aligned with the goals of the Foodies platform. One notable source of inspiration was the Brisbane City Council Food Truck website, which provides real-time information on food truck locations across the city. This project helped solidify the idea of integrating real-time data and interactive map features to improve user engagement.

Map page of Brisbane food truck (See Image 5). Reference: Brisbane City Council Food Truck, https://www.bnefoodtrucks.com.au/

Prototyping

During the prototyping phase, I took the lead in building the base mockup pages and setting up page navigation using JavaScript to ensure seamless transitions between different sections. After our team completed the mid-fidelity prototype, we gathered valuable user feedback that guided us in making a few key design changes. These adjustments helped refine the final prototype to better reflect our MVP features and enhance user experience.

The mid-fidelity prototype (see Image 6) represents the second stage of our design, incorporating feedback from early testing.

Additionally, I developed the backend in PHP to fetch data from the Food Truck and Market APIs, and I built the index page and details page. These pages display dynamic content pulled from the APIs, providing real-time information on food truck locations and events.

My commit history (see Image 7) from Github for our website's initial setup

Our final prototype website (see Image 8) showcases our MVP features, including API integration for food truck and market data, which was crucial to enhancing the site's functionality and user engagement.

Refinement

As the project progressed, I refined designs and improved user flows based on team feedback and testing results.

After multiple iterations, the design was refined into this final version (see Image 9).
